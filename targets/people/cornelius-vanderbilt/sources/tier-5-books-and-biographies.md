## The First Tycoon: The Epic Life of Cornelius Vanderbilt

- Author: T. J. Stiles
- Publisher: Alfred A. Knopf
- Publication date: 2009
- Edition: To be specified when physically or digitally consulted
- URL or reference: T. J. Stiles, *The First Tycoon: The Epic Life of Cornelius Vanderbilt*; Pulitzer Prize / National Book Award-winning biography
- Source type: Major modern biography

### Useful for

- Full Vanderbilt life narrative
- Early life and ferry career
- Steamboat and steamship career
- Railroad career
- Market power and corporate development
- Family and succession
- Character, reputation, and interpretation
- Correcting simplified robber-baron or hero narratives

### Claims supported

- Vanderbilt built wealth in shipping and railroads.
- Vanderbilt's career influenced the development of modern transportation capitalism and corporate power.
- Vanderbilt is a more complex figure than simple hero/villain accounts suggest.

### Documentation quality

High-priority biography. Reported to have won the National Book Award for Nonfiction and the Pulitzer Prize for Biography or Autobiography. It should become the primary narrative backbone until displaced by specific primary sources on individual claims.

### Reliability strengths

- Major modern biography.
- Prize-winning historical work.
- Useful for synthesis across Vanderbilt's entire life.
- Likely superior to encyclopedia summaries for interpretation and chronology.

### Reliability limits

- Still a secondary source.
- Interpretations should be cross-checked against primary records and other historians where possible.
- Page-level citations must be added before final professional reports are considered complete.

### Claims requiring cross-checking

- Specific financial values.
- Exact dates of transactions.
- Internal motives.
- Personality claims.
- Estate and legal claims.
- Railroad securities and market-conflict details.

### Notes

This should be the first full-length source to mine before expanding all Vanderbilt subtopic reports.
