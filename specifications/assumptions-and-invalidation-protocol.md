# Assumptions and Invalidation Protocol

## Purpose

This protocol defines how assumptions, dependency conditions, and invalidation triggers are recorded.

It prevents lessons and patterns from being transferred outside the conditions that made them work.

## Applies To

Use this protocol for:

- major claims;
- lessons with high transferability;
- candidate patterns;
- accepted patterns;
- strong patterns;
- principles.

## Required Fields

When material, record:

```text
assumptions
dependency_conditions
invalidation_triggers
```

## Assumptions

Assumptions are conditions believed to be true but not fully proven inside the object itself.

Examples:

- capital access was available;
- regulation allowed the tactic;
- technology conditions were stable;
- founder control was unusually high;
- labor conditions were historically specific;
- source survival is incomplete.

## Dependency Conditions

Dependency conditions are conditions required for the lesson or pattern to work.

Examples:

- scale economics exist;
- customer demand is durable;
- route or distribution control matters;
- financing remains available;
- operating discipline is present;
- law and ethics permit the behavior.

## Invalidation Triggers

Invalidation triggers are evidence or changed conditions that would weaken, retire, or reverse the object.

Examples:

- primary records contradict the claim;
- a stronger source changes the interpretation;
- counterexamples become more common than supporting cases;
- the tactic is illegal, unethical, or obsolete today;
- the pattern only works in one industry or era;
- the claimed causal mechanism is not supported.

## Transfer Rule

No lesson, pattern, or principle should be presented as broadly transferable without assumptions and limits.
