# Pattern Failure Conditions Protocol

## Purpose

This protocol defines how accepted patterns document where they work, where they fail, and where transfer is unsafe.

Patterns are not universal rules. They are historical regularities with conditions.

## Applies To

Required for:

- accepted patterns;
- strong patterns;
- candidate principles;
- accepted principles.

Recommended for high-confidence lessons.

## Required Pattern Fields

Every accepted pattern should document:

```text
success_conditions
failure_conditions
known_counterexamples
transferability_boundaries
assumptions
invalidation_triggers
competing_interpretations
time_horizon
```

## Success Conditions

Success conditions describe when the pattern appears to work.

Examples:

- durable customer demand;
- cost advantage remains real;
- regulation permits the behavior;
- capital access is sufficient;
- leadership can execute consistently;
- the market structure rewards the tactic.

## Failure Conditions

Failure conditions describe when the pattern breaks.

Examples:

- regulation changes;
- technology shifts;
- financing disappears;
- customer behavior changes;
- scale creates bureaucracy;
- founder control becomes succession risk;
- the tactic becomes illegal, unethical, or reputationally destructive.

## Transfer Rule

A pattern should not be copied as advice unless its failure conditions and transferability boundaries are visible.

## Principle Guard

No principle should be accepted from patterns that lack failure-condition analysis.
